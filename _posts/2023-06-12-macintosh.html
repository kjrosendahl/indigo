--- title: macintosh layout: post date: 2023-06-12 23:42 category: poetry description: "when productivity feels foreign" author: kjros ---

I changed the wallpaper of my MacBook Air with its M1 chip and 8 GB RAM to match yours. It's the one you asked my opinion on: the pale sun rising on a virtual beach transforming in the background of cluster deployments until the rocks and waves cast shadows of dark fuschia and royal purple, a fantasy land.

I configure my screens in perfect order, notes to labs to the gcloud CLI with the flick of my wrist, and my thoughts reach my fingers, the files, like lightning, the keys click-clacking sharp electric instantaneous.

I am suddenly you - eyes darting to the next task never staying still for a moment, servant to the screen, face aglow in blue light, instant gratification in the form of pop-up notifications.

But even as I mirror this mastery (the computer) (the terminal) (the IDE) I am not you. I cannot locate that cool confidence you charmed me with, nor the intuition only years of curiosity can grant.

Forever in the shadow of your brilliance (always Adam, never Eve) (always Eve, never the apple) I stand, waiting, for clarity.
