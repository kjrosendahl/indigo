Why make a website?

My academic advisor mentioned in my second year how I should have a website to showcase my projects and a little about myself. I pondered this for a while; I have always wanted a personal site, reminiscent of the era of MySpace and IM.

The thing is, I wanted total control, and this meant designing anything I wanted. I had used website builders in high school, but they didn't have quite the same freedom I was seeking. I wanted to create something I could be proud of, understand what was going behind the scenes, and have fun!

So, I doubled down and started studying front-end design. HTML, CSS, a little JavaSript: the works. You can see the result here! I did use a Jekyll theme, but I'm still parsing and exploring the files. It was through this project I found my interest in front-end engineering. After graduation, I hope to continue following this passion.

I'm pretty sure my advisor was suggesting something along the lines of a Google site, but I've never been one to color inside the lines.
