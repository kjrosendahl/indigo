I'm not the biggest cinephile on the planet - it's hard for me to sit through a movie and not get distracted.

Regardless, there are a few movies I consider better than others. Read more about them here!

1. Clue (1985)

Genre: mystery, black comedy

13 year old me loved this movie; 21 year old me loves it even more. Based on the game, six strangers are invited to mysterious dinner party. The one thing they have in common? They're all being blackmailed. Of course, things go a little awry when the lights go out and their host, the blackmailer, is murdered. With dark humor and double entendres, this film captured my heart.

2. Spider-Man: Into the Spider-verse (2018)

Genre: action, adventure, animated

Perhaps the most gorgeously animated movie in history. Watching Into the Spider-verse on the big screen left me breathless: the vivid color schemes, lines and hatches, blends of art styles, double-vision frames, electricity, crisp motions... Not only is it visually stunning, but it shattered my expectations for being a mere 'animated movie.' I will never tire of watching it.

3. Knives Out (2019), Glass Onion (2022)

Genre: mystery

Whether detective Benoit Blanc is investigating a suspicious death and a dysfunctional family, or a murder on a private remote island, one thing ties the cases together: an outlandish Southern accent. Both Knives Out and its standalone sequel, The Glass Onion, boast colorful ensemble casts, witty jokes, and twisting mysteries. Can't wait for the third film.

4. Baby Driver (2017)

Genre: action, crime drama

Because who can function without the perfect playlist? Follow Baby, a near-mute getaway driver sucked into a life of crime to pay off his debts. He uses music to escape this morally gray area, and if you're anything like me? You'll find Baby awkwardly charismatic and the soundtrack even more so.

5. Coraline (2009)

Genre: stop motion, dark fantasy, horror

Coraline is a girl seeking excitement - so why shouldn't she travel through a portal to the Other World, where its occupants have sewn buttons for eyes? Despite being scarred for life, my sister and I watched this movie at least every two weeks as kids. With lovingly-created stop-motion animation and a compelling story, I highly recommend - as long as you're not scared of the dark.

6. (500) Days of Summer (2009)

Genre: rom-com, drama

In a non-linear timeline, discover the beginning, middle, and end of the relationship between Tom and the eponymous Summer. One is a hopeless romantic, while the other doesn't believe in love. I found this film bittersweet, reminiscient of a time I can no longer remember. And yet, I was still drawn to it, despite how heartbreaking.

7. Good Will Hunting (1997)

Genre: drama

Troubled, brilliant, cocky, misguided - who is Will Hunting? Haunted by his past and self-sabotaging, Will finds himself head-to-head with a psychologist determined to help him. I love this movie for its power: monologues and quips, introspection and quietly shifting moments. Try it out; you'll never be the same.
